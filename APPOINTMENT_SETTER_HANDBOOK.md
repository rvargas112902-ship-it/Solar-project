# Door-to-Door Solar Appointment Setter Handbook

## 0) Purpose
Your job is simple: **start quality conversations and set qualified appointments that show and close.**

This handbook is your daily operating system. Follow it exactly for 30 days before you "customize" anything.

---

## 1) Winning Mindset (Non-Negotiable)

### Identity
- You are not "pitching random strangers."
- You are a **local energy consultant** helping homeowners evaluate savings and reliability.
- Your product on the door is not solar panels. It is a **10-15 minute decision meeting**.

### Mental rules
1. **Detach from immediate outcomes.** One "no" means nothing.
2. **Control controllables:** doors knocked, conversations started, quality asks, follow-up.
3. **Momentum over perfection.** Fast action beats perfect wording.
4. **Professional optimism.** You expect resistance and stay composed.

### 10-second reset script (between doors)
> "Next door, fresh start. Smile, posture, pace. Ask one good question and earn the appointment."

---

## 2) Daily Expectations & Activity Standards

### Baseline daily standards (new rep)
- **Doors knocked:** 80-120
- **Conversations (decision-maker):** 20-35
- **Full pitches:** 8-15
- **Qualified appointments set:** 2-5
- **Appointments that show (weekly):** 60%+ target

### Core KPI formula
`Knocks -> Conversations -> Pitches -> Appointments Set -> Appointments Shown -> Closed Deals`

Improve by fixing the **earliest weak conversion point** first.

### Simple daily schedule
1. **Pre-field prep (30 min):** script reps, objections, territory plan.
2. **Field block 1 (2-3 hrs):** high focus, no phone scrolling.
3. **Midday review (15 min):** conversion check, adjust opener/qualifier.
4. **Field block 2 (2-3 hrs):** peak energy zone.
5. **Evening follow-up (30 min):** confirmations, reschedules, notes.

## 4) Body Language & Tonality That Converts

### Body language do's
- Upright posture, shoulders open
- Slight forward lean when listening
- Nod when they speak
- Use small, clear hand gestures

### Tonality framework
Use three tones intentionally:
1. **Warm tone** (intro): lowers guard  
2. **Confident tone** (authority): builds trust  
3. **Curious tone** (questions): keeps engagement

### Tempo control
- Slow down on key points ("This takes about 15 minutes.")
- Pause after questions (let them think)
- Never "machine-gun" your script

## 5) Door Approach & Pitch Structure (Field-Proven)

## The 8-step doorstep system
1. **Pattern interrupt**
2. **Credibility statement**
3. **Reason for stopping by**
4. **Micro-qualification question**
5. **Value hook**
6. **Soft assumption**
7. **Appointment ask**
8. **Calendar lock + confirmation**

### Core script (memorize first, personalize later)
> "Hey, I'm [Name] with [Company]. I'll be quick.  
> We're helping a few homeowners on this street review utility costs and solar eligibility before rates change again.  
> Quick question - are you the homeowner here?  
> (If yes) Perfect. Most people we meet want to see if they qualify for lower fixed power costs without changing much up front.  
> Our specialist is already in the area doing 10-15 minute evaluations.  
> Would today at 6:15 or 7:00 be better?"

### Why this works
- Low-pressure opener
- Local relevance
- Decision-maker filter
- Specific appointment windows
- Assumptive but respectful close

## 6) Objection Handling Framework

## Use LACE
1. **Listen** fully (don't interrupt)
2. **Acknowledge** ("Totally fair.")
3. **Clarify** with one question
4. **Engage** with a short response + re-ask

Keep rebuttals under 20 seconds.

### Common objections + scripts

#### "Not interested."
> "Totally fair - most people say that before they see actual numbers.  
> Out of curiosity, is it solar in general, or just bad timing today?"

(After response)
> "Makes sense. That's exactly why we do a quick review first.  
> Let's do a short 15-minute look so you can decide with real data.  
> Would 6:15 or 7:00 be better?"

#### "I'm busy."
> "I hear you. That's why we keep it to 10-15 minutes, max.  
> Better later today or tomorrow?"

#### "I need to talk to my spouse."
> "100% - both decision-makers should be there.  
> What time are you both usually home so we can do one clean conversation together?"

#### "Just leave me info."
> "Happy to. Most people find the info too general without seeing their own usage and roof fit.  
> Let's put 15 minutes on the calendar and I'll leave the summary after.  
> Is evening or midday better?"

#### "We already looked into solar."
> "Good - then you already know what to compare.  
> Programs and rates change often, so this is just an updated comparison.  
> If numbers are worse, you walk. Fair?"

## 7) Lead Qualification: Who Gets an Appointment

Use the **S.O.L.A.R.** filter before scheduling:

- **S - Homeowner status** (decision-maker present)
- **O - Ownership timeline** (staying at least 2+ years preferred)
- **L - Light bill relevance** (meaningful monthly electricity usage)
- **A - Authority alignment** (all decision-makers can attend)
- **R - Roof / property fit indicators** (or open to ground options where applicable)

If one key item is missing, either:
- book as "needs verification," or
- disqualify politely and move on.

### Quick qualifier script
> "Just so I don't waste your time - you own the home, and both decision-makers can be there for the quick review, right?"

---

## 8) Scheduling Appointments That Actually Show

### Appointment-setting process (step-by-step)
1. Offer **two specific times** (not "what works?")
2. Confirm all decision-makers attending
3. Confirm address and best phone number
4. Set expectation: "10-15 minutes"
5. Add calendar invite/text immediately
6. Same-day reminder + 30-minute reminder

### Lock-in script
> "Perfect, I have you for **today at 6:15 PM** at **[address]** with **[spouse name]** joining.  
> This is a quick 10-15 minute eligibility and savings review.  
> I'll send a confirmation text now - what's the best number?"

### Confirmation text template
> "Hi [Name], this is [Rep] with [Company]. Confirming your solar savings review at [Time] today at [Address]. We'll keep it to ~15 min. Please have all decision-makers present. Reply YES to confirm."

## 9) Handling Rejection Without Losing Performance

### The rejection truth
- Rejection is feedback on **timing/message fit**, not your value as a person.
- Elite setters hear "no" constantly and still win because they keep volume and quality high.

### 3-step rejection recovery
1. **Physical reset:** exhale, shoulders down, smile
2. **Micro-review:** "Where did I lose control? opener, qualifier, or ask?"
3. **Immediate action:** next door in under 20 seconds

No emotional carryover between houses.

## 11) Follow-Up System (Most Reps Skip This)

### Required follow-up cadence
- **Immediately after set:** confirmation text
- **Day of appointment (morning):** reminder
- **30-60 min before:** final confirmation
- **No-show:** reschedule attempt within 10 minutes, then again in 24 hours

### No-show reschedule script
> "Hey [Name], we missed you today - no problem, schedules get busy.  
> Let's get you rebooked for a quick 15-minute review.  
> Would tomorrow at 6:00 or 6:45 be better?"

## 12) Roleplay Scenarios (Train Daily)

Run each roleplay for 5 minutes, switch roles, repeat until smooth.

### Scenario 1: Fast brush-off
- Homeowner says: "Not interested."
- Goal: Use LACE and re-ask with two time options.
- Win condition: calm tone, no arguing, appointment ask made.

### Scenario 2: Time objection
- Homeowner says: "I don't have time."
- Goal: compress to 10-15 minute value proposition.
- Win condition: secure a later same-day slot.

### Scenario 3: Spouse gatekeeper
- Homeowner says: "My husband/wife handles this."
- Goal: include all decision-makers and lock joint time.
- Win condition: both names + attendance confirmed.

### Scenario 4: Skeptic homeowner
- Homeowner says: "Solar is a scam."
- Goal: acknowledge, ask one clarifying question, position meeting as data-only.
- Win condition: homeowner agrees to compare numbers.
