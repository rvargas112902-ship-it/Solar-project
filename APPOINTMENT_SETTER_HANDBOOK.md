# Elite Appointment Setter Handbook
## Door-to-Door Solar Team Operating System

**Outcome:** More sits, cleaner handoffs, higher close rate.

---

## Mission and Standard

This system is built for **field execution**, not theory.

- Build trust quickly.
- Create local relevance immediately.
- Qualify with bill pain early.
- Lock a clear next step every time.

---

## Daily Team Minimums

- **80 doors/day** minimum
- **2 sits or 1 sale/day** minimum
- Track: doors, conversations, bill pulls, sits, sales

Top reps do not guess. They track, adjust, and improve daily.

---

## Setter Identity

You are not a technical explainer.  
You are a **decision guide**.

Lead with what homeowners already feel:

- outages
- rate increases
- monthly bill pressure
- paying more for same usage

---

## The Four Non-Negotiables at the Door

1. Lower resistance in the first 5 seconds.
2. Make the reason local and practical.
3. Confirm fit through bill conversation.
4. Move to sit-now or appointment lock.

---

## Daily Field Rhythm

**Before knocking**

1. Confirm turf and route.
2. Review opener, bill question, close.
3. Set targets for doors and conversations.
4. Confirm handoff expectations.

---

## Daily Field Rhythm (Execution)

**In field**
- Keep pace between doors.
- Check metrics every 25 doors.
- Reset instantly after rejection.

**End of day**
- Report full scorecard.
- Pick one skill for tomorrow reps.

---

## First-Impression Psychology

Homeowners decide fast: threat, nuisance, or normal person.

Mechanics that lower guard:
- half-step back after knock
- hands visible
- slight angle stance
- quick natural smile
- speak first, avoid awkward silence

---

## Core Pitch Framework (9 Lines)

1. Time release
2. Local anchor
3. Local reason
4. Micro-agreement
5. Rate bridge
6. Shared frustration
7. Conditional value
8. Disqualification
9. Bill question

---

## Full Core Pitch Script

> "Hey listen, I do not have much time.  
> Do you see these powerlines here?  
> They are going underground after storm outages.  
> Does that make sense?  
> Duke is increasing rates to pay for that work, and no one likes paying more for the same power.  
> If we can get you that same power cheaper, we will show you exact numbers.  
> Most neighbors are only at $35-$40, so we probably cannot help them.  
> What is the lowest your Duke bill gets?"

---

## Why Line 1 Works

**"I do not have much time."**

- Pattern interrupt
- Signals short conversation
- Creates calm authority

Delivery:
- quick and casual
- never rushed
- never theatrical

---

## Why Lines 2-4 Work

**Local anchor + local reason + micro-agreement**

- Moves conversation from random pitch to neighborhood context
- Creates shared focus (you and homeowner look at same thing)
- Gets first "yes" early with: **"Does that make sense?"**

---

## Why Lines 5-7 Work

**Rate bridge -> pain -> conditional value**

- Connects infrastructure to monthly bill
- Creates "us vs rising costs" frame
- Uses low-risk language: **"if we can..."**

Result: curiosity, not pressure.

---

## Why Lines 8-9 Work

**Disqualification + bill question**

- Signals not everyone is a fit
- Reduces sales pressure
- Pulls real numbers early

Ask with neutral curiosity:
**"What is the lowest your Duke bill gets?"**

---

## Sound Human, Not Scripted

- Keep order, but vary connector words
- Acknowledge reactions before next line
- Match homeowner pace
- Stay brief with guarded personalities
- Slow down with skeptics

Use natural bridges: "Exactly," "Fair," "Got it."

---

## Rapid Rapport Formula

1. Release pressure  
2. Use local reference  
3. Get micro-agreement  
4. Share frustration  
5. Ask fit question

Rapport means: **safe enough to keep talking.**

---

## Tonality System

Core tones:
- calm authority
- local concern
- curious investigator
- assumptive logistics (during close)

Avoid:
- too loud
- too fast
- too excited
- one-tone robot voice

---

## Conversation Control

Use short questions to climb micro-agreements:

1. "See these powerlines?"
2. "Does that make sense?"
3. "Notice your bill moving up?"
4. "Lowest bill?"
5. "Summer peak?"

Each answer earns the next question.

---

## Transitions That Keep Control

Use:
- "Real quick..."
- "Out of curiosity..."
- "Before I waste your time..."

Do not ask:
- "Are you interested in solar?"
- "What do you think about solar?"
- "When are you free?"

---

## Pain-Building Framework

**Surface -> Magnify -> Personalize -> Contrast**

1. Lowest bill?
2. Summer spike?
3. Why that range?
4. Would predictable/lower be worth a look?

Ask one question at a time. Never interrogate.

---

## Bill Transition

Primary line:

> "Perfect. Grab your latest Duke bill real quick and I will show you in 60 seconds if this is even a fit."

If resistance:

> "Totally fair. Without the bill we would just be guessing."

---

## Usage Offset Explanation

Keep it visual and short:

1. Point to usage trend on bill.
2. "This is your monthly usage pattern."
3. "Goal is to offset usage so you buy less from Duke."
4. "Not less power use, just lower cost for same use."

---

## Qualification Rules

**Likely good fit**
- bill above local threshold
- decision-makers involved
- viable roof/site
- frustration with rising rates

**Low-priority lead**
- consistent $35-$40 range
- non-decision-maker
- no urgency or bill pain

---

## Sit-Now vs Appointment

If both homeowners are available and qualified:
- move to sit now

If one is missing:
- lock two-homeowner appointment

Positioning line:
> "Let us do this once with both of you there."

---

## Appointment Closing System

1. Decision-maker check
2. Set "both must be present" frame
3. Give two exact time options
4. Confirm commitment out loud
5. Secure contact + bill photo
6. Reduce no-show risk with reconfirm line

---

## Final Lock Line

> "Awesome, this is just a numbers walkthrough to see if it saves you money.  
> If it does not, we shake hands and you are done."

Clear, low pressure, high certainty.

---

## ARC Objection Framework

**A - Acknowledge**  
"Fair."

**R - Reframe**  
"That is why we look at your real usage first."

**C - Close (micro-commitment)**  
"If it saves money, worth 10 minutes to verify?"

---

## Objection Loops (High Frequency)

**"Not interested."**  
"Totally fair. Most people are interested in paying less than Duke. Are you open to seeing the numbers?"

**"No time."**  
"Same here. Give me 60 seconds at the door, then we decide."

Always finish with a forward question.

---

## Objection Loops (Advanced)

**"Need to talk to spouse."**  
"Exactly. That is why we only meet when both are present. Tomorrow 5:45 or 6:30?"

**"Solar is a scam."**  
"Fair concern. We use your real bill and transparent numbers first."

**"Roof is old/shaded."**  
"Could disqualify you. Let us verify fit first."

---

## Example Flow: Qualified Homeowner

1. Opener + local anchor
2. Storm/rate context
3. Shared frustration
4. Lowest + highest bill questions
5. Bill pull
6. Sit-now or locked appointment

The structure is predictable. Delivery stays conversational.

---

## Example Flow: Hard Brush-Off

Homeowner: "Not interested."

Setter:
1. Acknowledge: "Totally fair."
2. Reframe: "Most people just want lower than Duke."
3. Micro-close: "Usually under $50 or over $150?"

Keep calm. Keep moving forward.

---

## Roleplay Training Block (Daily 20 Minutes)

1. 5 min opener reps
2. 5 min "why each line" reps
3. 5 min pain-build reps
4. 5 min objection speed round

Goal: ARC response in under 4 seconds.

---

## Weekly Practice Scenarios

- busy homeowner
- skeptic ("solar is a scam")
- friendly low-bill homeowner
- one spouse absent
- prior bad quote comparison
- interruption during opener

Score: delivery, rapport, control, clarity, close.

---

## Top Setter Mistakes (And Fixes)

1. Too much talking before qualification -> ask bill sooner
2. Robotic delivery -> respond before next line
3. Product before pain -> bill pressure first
4. Open-ended close -> always give two time options
5. Skipping decision-maker check -> confirm early

---

## Conversion Best Practices

- Keep door conversations short and directional.
- Use local references.
- Ask better questions instead of longer explanations.
- Listen for emotional cues.
- Pause after important bill numbers.
- Confirm commitments out loud.
- Send confirmation text before leaving.

---

## Setter-to-Closer Handoff Checklist

- homeowner names
- best phone number
- decision-maker status
- low/high monthly bill range
- emotional pain trigger
- surfaced objections
- appointment lock time
- bill photo secured

---

## Handoff Example (Tight and Useful)

> "They are at $160 low and $300 high.  
> Biggest pain is summer unpredictability.  
> Both homeowners confirmed for 5:45.  
> Concern was scam risk, but they are open to transparent numbers."

---

## Rejection Recovery Protocol

1. Label the outcome: brush-off, objection, or bad fit.
2. Capture one lesson (if any).
3. Move to next door within 20 seconds.

Measure execution, not emotion.
