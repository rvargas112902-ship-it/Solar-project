## 1. The Job in One Sentence

**You are paid to put qualified homeowners on a calendar with a solar consultant.**

You are not paid to:

- Educate the entire neighborhood.
- Close the deal at the door.
- Argue.
- Convince skeptics.

You are paid to **find the few who are open, qualify them in under 5 minutes, and lock a firm time on the calendar with both decision-makers present.**

Everything in this handbook serves that one outcome.

## 2. Mindset

### The Why

Doors don't beat reps. Reps beat themselves. 90% of failure on the doors is mental.

### The Four Beliefs of a Top Setter

1. **"The next door is the one."** Every "no" pays you, because it gets you closer to the "yes."
2. **"I'm doing them a favor."** You're offering lower bills, cleaner energy, and equity. That's a gift, not an interruption.
3. **"Rejection is not personal."** They aren't rejecting you. They are rejecting a stranger at their door. You haven't been introduced yet.
4. **"I control the controllables."** Doors knocked, attitude, energy, script. Nothing else.

### The Math That Sets You Free

In most markets:

- **100 doors knocked** ≈ 25 conversations
- **25 conversations** ≈ 3–5 appointments
- **3–5 appointments** ≈ 1 install

That means **every door is worth roughly $2–$5 in commission whether they open or not**. Knock it like it's already paid.

### Daily Mental Reps

- **Morning (5 min):** Look in the mirror. Say out loud: "I'm the best part of this homeowner's day."
- **Pre-knock (3 sec):** Take one deep breath, smile, then knock.
- **After a rough door (10 sec):** Walk it off. Reset posture. Next door, fresh face.
- **End of day (5 min):** Write down 3 wins, 1 lesson, tomorrow's #.

## 4. Appearance

### The Why

The homeowner decides if they trust you in **the first 3 seconds**. 93% of that decision is visual. You will never out-talk a bad first impression.

### Standard Uniform

- Branded polo or button-up, tucked in.
- Clean dark pants or khakis – no shorts, no joggers.
- Closed-toe shoes, clean. No flip-flops, no scuffed sneakers.
- Company ID badge on lanyard, visible at chest height.
- iPad in hand or under arm – signals "professional," not "salesman."
- Hat optional, but **off when the door opens**.

### Grooming Checklist (Before Leaving the Car)

- [ ] Hair neat
- [ ] Facial hair trimmed
- [ ] Breath fresh (mints in pocket)
- [ ] Nails clean
- [ ] No strong cologne/perfume
- [ ] No sunglasses on when the door opens
- [ ] Phone on silent

### The "Neighbor Test"

Ask yourself: *Would my mom open the door for me dressed like this?* If the answer is no, fix it before you knock.

## 5. Body Language

### The Why

Posture talks before your mouth does. The homeowner reads your body in milliseconds and decides: *threat or friend?*

### The 4-Step Doorstep Stance

1. **Stand at a 45° angle to the door.** Never square-up – that's how cops and salesmen stand. Angled = friendly neighbor.
2. **Take one step back after knocking.** Gives them space. Reduces the "stranger at my door" reflex.
3. **Hands visible, relaxed at waist or holding iPad.** Never in pockets. Never crossed.
4. **Smile before they open.** The smile must already be on your face when the door cracks.

### Micro-Signals That Build Trust

- **Eye contact:** 70% of the time. Break it occasionally to glance at their house ("Nice place, by the way").
- **Open palms** when gesturing – never point fingers.
- **Nodding** while they speak – shows you're listening.
- **Mirror their energy.** Calm homeowner → calm you. Chatty homeowner → warmer you.

### Red Flags to Eliminate

- Fidgeting with phone, pen, or paperwork.
- Looking down at iPad while pitching.
- Standing too close (closer than arm's length).
- Talking over them.
- Backing away too early when they object.

## 6. Tonality

### The Why

**Words are 7%. Tone is 38%. Body is 55%.** You can say the right script in the wrong tone and lose every time.

### The Three Tones You Need

1. **Curious Neighbor Tone** (opening): Light, upbeat, slightly rising at the end. As if you're asking for directions, not selling.
   - *"Hey, real quick – are you the homeowner here?"*

2. **Authority Tone** (qualifying & scheduling): Calm, even, slightly lower pitch, statements not questions. This is a doctor's voice, not a salesman's voice.
   - *"What we're going to do is have our energy consultant come out Thursday at 6."*

3. **Empathy Tone** (objections): Slow, soft, matching their pace. You're a friend, not a closer.
   - *"Totally get it... a lot of folks on this street said the exact same thing at first."*

### The Power of the Pause

- After your opener: **pause 1 second**. Let them respond.
- After an objection: **pause 2 seconds**, then answer. The pause = confidence.
- Before naming the appointment time: **pause**. It tells their brain "this is important."

### Tonality Drills

- Record yourself reading the pitch every morning for 5 minutes. Listen back. Cut the "ums."
- Practice the same line three ways: question, statement, command.
- Read children's books out loud with full expression. (Seriously. Top reps do this.)

## 7. Pitch Structure (The 5-Step Knock)

This is your default knock. Memorize it. Don't deviate until you've set 50 appointments.

### Step 1 – Pattern Interrupt (5 sec)

Goal: Don't sound like a salesman in the first sentence.

> **"Hey, how's it going? — Sorry to bug you. I'm [NAME] with [COMPANY], I'm not selling anything door-to-door — I'm actually the guy your neighbors keep pointing at when I drive through here. Are you the homeowner?"**

Why it works:
- "Sorry to bug you" disarms.
- "Not selling anything" lowers the wall (technically true – you're scheduling, not selling).
- "Your neighbors keep pointing" = social proof.
- "Are you the homeowner?" = first qualifying question.

### Step 2 – Reason for the Visit (15 sec)

> **"So the reason I'm out here — the utility company just rolled out a new program with the state where homeowners on [STREET / ZIP] can lock in their electric rate and stop the rate hikes that have been happening every year. I'm literally just going door to door letting people know how it works — takes about 30 seconds, cool?"**

Why it works:
- Mentions a "program" – sounds like a benefit, not a product.
- Names the utility and street – local credibility.
- Asks for 30 seconds – low commitment.

### Step 3 – Micro-Pitch (45 sec)

> **"Basically, instead of renting power from [UTILITY] forever and watching your bill go up 4–6% a year, you can own your power at a fixed rate — typically the same or lower than what you're paying now, with $0 out of pocket. The state's covering 30% of it through a tax credit. Three of your neighbors on [STREET] already got approved — that's why I'm out here."**

The four ingredients:
1. **Problem:** rising bills.
2. **Solution:** own your power, fixed rate.
3. **Proof:** neighbors, tax credit.
4. **Risk-reversal:** $0 out of pocket.

### Step 4 – Qualifying Questions (60 sec)

Ask all four. Don't skip any.

1. *"Quick question — about how much are you paying [UTILITY] on an average month?"* (Looking for $100+.)
2. *"You guys own the home, right? How long have you been here?"* (Looking for 2+ years, plan to stay.)
3. *"Roof's in decent shape? No leaks, not super old?"* (Looking for 10 years or less.)
4. *"Last thing — when something like this comes up, is it just you, or you and your [spouse/partner] making the call together?"* (Both decision-makers must be at the appointment.)

### Step 5 – Lock the Appointment (60 sec)

Assume the appointment. Do not ask permission.

> **"Cool, here's what makes sense — our energy consultant is going to be in the neighborhood [DAY] anyway because of the [neighbor]. He'll pull up your actual usage from [UTILITY], show you what your savings look like, and answer any questions. Takes about 30–45 minutes, both you and [spouse] need to be there. Does [TIME OPTION A] work, or is [TIME OPTION B] better?"**

The keys:
- **Alternate close** – two times, not yes/no.
- **Both spouses required** – non-negotiable.
- **Frame it as informational** – not a sales pitch.
- **Set expectations** – 30–45 minutes.

## 8. Lead Qualification

A bad appointment costs the company money and steals your time. Qualify ruthlessly.

### The 5 Qualifiers (BANT+R)

| Letter | Meaning | What to confirm |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| **B** | Bill | $100/month minimum (varies by market) |
| **A** | Authority | Both decision-makers will attend |
| **N** | Need | Bills are going up, they care |
| **T** | Timing | Live there 2+ years, plan to stay 5+ |
| **R** | Roof | Less than 10 yrs old, no major shade, owns the home |

### Disqualify These Leads (Don't Set the Appointment)

- Renters.
- Bill under $80/month (no real savings story).
- Roof needs full replacement and they refuse to discuss it.
- Plan to sell within 12 months.
- One spouse refuses to be present.
- Already have solar (note for referrals, then move on).
- Active bankruptcy / credit issues volunteered by them.

### Soft Qualifiers (Still Set, But Note in CRM)

- Older roof (note age, consultant will assess).
- HOA concerns.
- Tree shade (note where).
- Heavily skeptical but agreed.

### The Honest Rule

If they wouldn't qualify on paper, **don't set the appointment**. A canceled appointment hurts you more than a missed pitch. Your job is quality, not just quantity.

## 9. Scheduling Appointments

### The Why

The appointment is the product. Setting it cleanly is your only KPI that matters.

### The 5 Rules of a Locked Appointment

1. **Both decision-makers confirmed by name.** *"So I've got John AND Sarah, Thursday at 6 — that's correct?"*
2. **Time within 72 hours.** The further out, the more cancels. Same-week appointments hold 2x better.
3. **Avoid 4:00–5:30 PM slots** – dinner / kid pickup chaos = no-shows.
4. **Send the calendar invite + text from your phone before you leave the doorstep.** They watch you do it. Cancellation rate drops in half.
5. **Confirm the day before with a text, not a call.** Texts get a response. Calls get ignored.

### The Confirmation Script (At the Door)

> **"Perfect — I'm putting you in right now. What's the best cell to reach you? ... Cool, I'm texting you my contact and the calendar invite right now — go ahead and save my number as '[Name] – Solar Guy.' [Consultant Name] will text you about an hour before he heads over Thursday. Anything come up between now and then, you text me directly. Sound good?"**

### The 24-Hour Confirmation Text

> *"Hey [Name], it's [You] with [Company]. Just confirming [Consultant] is stopping by tomorrow ([Day]) at [Time] to go over your energy savings — you and [Spouse] both good? Reply YES to lock it in."*

If they don't reply YES, **call them**. No reply = no appointment.

## 10. Objection Handling

### The Why

Objections are not "no." Objections are **requests for more information** wrapped in fear. Your job: acknowledge → reframe → redirect to the appointment.

### The Universal Formula: FEEL → FELT → FOUND

> **"I totally get how you feel. A lot of your neighbors felt the same way. What they found was..."**

This is the Swiss Army knife. Use it on anything you haven't memorized a response for yet.

### The Top 12 Objections + Responses

#### 1. "I'm not interested."

> *"Totally fair — most people say that before they know what it is. That's actually why I'm just dropping info, not selling anything today. Real quick — is it the solar part you're not into, or just door-knockers in general?"*

(Their answer tells you whether to keep going or politely exit.)

#### 2. "I'm busy / Bad time."

> *"100% — I'm not here to keep you. I literally just need 30 seconds, and if it doesn't make sense I'm out of your hair. Cool?"*

#### 3. "Send me something in the mail."

> *"I would, but the program's specific to your house's roof and your bill — that's why we send a consultant out, not a brochure. Takes 30 minutes. Worst case, you save zero. Best case, you knock a couple hundred bucks off your bill. Thursday or Friday work better?"*

#### 4. "I rent."

> *"Got it, totally fine — appreciate your time. Real quick before I go, do you know the homeowner's name or any neighbors on the street who own?"*

(Move on, get referrals.)

#### 5. "I already have solar."

> *"Oh nice — who'd you go with? ... Cool, are you happy with the production? ... [Listen.] We actually do referrals and sometimes battery add-ons for existing systems — mind if I have someone reach out?"*

(Note in CRM, move on.)

#### 6. "I'm not the homeowner / It's my parents' house."

> *"Got it — are they around, or what's the best time to catch them? I'm out here Tuesdays and Thursdays."*

#### 7. "My roof is too old / I'm getting a new roof."

> *"That's actually perfect timing — a lot of folks bundle the roof with solar because part of the cost becomes tax-deductible. The consultant can walk you through how that works. When's good — Thursday or Friday?"*

#### 8. "I can't afford it."

> *"That's why I led with $0 out of pocket — there's no upfront cost. You're literally just trading a power bill for a lower, fixed bill. If it doesn't save you money, you don't do it. Worth 30 minutes to see the numbers?"*

#### 9. "Solar is a scam / I've heard bad things."

> *"I hear that a lot, and honestly — there are some shady companies out there. That's exactly why I want you to meet our consultant. He's going to show you the actual contract, the production guarantee in writing, and the local installs. You can shut it down in 5 minutes if it smells off. Fair?"*

#### 10. "I need to think about it / Talk to my spouse."

> *"Smart — never make a decision without [spouse]. That's exactly why the appointment includes both of you. We're not asking you to decide today — we're just getting the info in front of both of you at the same time. What night this week are you guys both home?"*

#### 11. "Just leave me your card."

> *"I would, but honestly cards end up in the junk drawer. Tell you what — let me grab your number, I'll text you mine, and if you want me to come back I will. Cool?"*

(If they still insist on card-only, leave one. Note for follow-up.)

#### 12. "How did you get my address? / Are you with the utility?"

> *"Good question — no, I'm not with [UTILITY], I work with [COMPANY], we're the local installer. I'm just walking the street — no list, no database, just door to door. Promise."*

### The Three-Strike Rule

If you've FEEL-FELT-FOUND **three times** and they're still saying no, **politely exit**. Don't be that rep. Drop a card, get a referral name if you can, and go to the next door.

> *"Totally respect that — appreciate your time. If your bill ever spikes, my number's on the card. Have a good one."*

## 11. Handling Rejection

### The Why

You will be told "no" 80–95 times out of 100. If "no" damages you, you will not survive this job. If "no" energizes you, you will get rich.

### Reframes That Actually Work

- **"No" = "Not yet."** Half the people who say no this week will buy from someone within a year. Plant the seed.
- **"No" is a coin flip with money.** Every door is a paid lottery ticket. Knock fast, knock many.
- **They're saying no to a stranger, not to you.** They don't know you yet.
- **The best reps get rejected the most.** Volume is the moat.

### The 60-Second Reset Routine

After a rough door:

1. **Walk 10 steps** before you stop to journal. Movement clears cortisol.
2. **Big inhale, slow exhale.** Twice.
3. **Smile** – even fake. The brain follows the face.
4. **Say out loud:** *"Next one."*
5. **Knock within 60 seconds.** Do not sit in the truck. Do not scroll.

### The "Slammed Door" Protocol

When someone genuinely slams the door or is rude:

1. Smile, say *"Have a good night."* (Loud enough they hear.)
2. Walk away calmly. Do not flinch, do not mutter.
3. Next door — keep the same energy you had before. Do **not** carry the last door's energy into the next pitch.

### Weekly Rejection Audit

Every Sunday, ask yourself:

- Did I take a "no" personally this week? Which one?
- What did that door teach me?
- What would I say differently if I knocked it again?

Top reps treat every "no" like film study.

## 12. Discipline

### The Why

Talent gets you to average. Discipline gets you to top 1%. This job pays the disciplined, not the gifted.

### The 10 Non-Negotiables of an Elite Setter

1. **In the field by start time. Every day.**
2. **Last door knocked at 8:30 PM. Every day.**
3. **No phone-scrolling between doors.**
4. **CRM updated before bed. Every night.**
5. **Pre-shift roleplay. Every morning.**
6. **No complaining about leads, weather, or area.**
7. **No headphones in while walking.** (You miss the homeowner's hello.)
8. **No drinking the night before a shift.**
9. **No skipping the "ugly" houses.** They convert.
10. **No leaving the field early because you "got one."**

### The Habits That Compound

- **Wake at the same time daily.** Sleep is performance gear.
- **Hydrate** – 1 gallon water per day in the field.
- **Cardio 3x/week.** This job is physical.
- **Read 10 pages of a sales/mindset book per night.**
- **Track every door** in your knock app. What gets measured improves.

### The Field Routine (Every Single Door)

1. Park 2 streets away – get reps walking.
2. Plan the block – who's home, who's lights on.
3. Approach. Smile. Knock. Step back.
4. Pitch. Qualify. Set or exit cleanly.
5. Log the door immediately on your phone.
6. Walk to the next door **within 30 seconds**.

## 13. Follow-Up

### The Why

**80% of setters quit after the first "no."** **80% of money is in the follow-up.** Be the rep that comes back.

### The Categories

| Lead Type | When to follow up | How |
|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| **Set appointment** | 24 hrs before | Text + call if no reply |
| **No-show** | Same day, 1 hr after missed time | Text: "Hey, missed you – everything okay?" |
| **Not home** | 48 hrs later, different time of day | Knock again |
| **"Come back later"** | Exact time they said | Knock |
| **Soft no / interested but timing** | 2 weeks | Text + drop-by |
| **Hard no** | Quarterly | Drop a card or referral postcard |
| **Existing solar customer** | 30 days | Ask for referral |

### The Follow-Up Text Templates

**Set appointment (24 hr confirm):**
> *"Hey [Name], [You] with [Company]. Confirming [Consultant] tomorrow at [Time] — you and [Spouse] both good? Reply YES."*

**Missed appointment:**
> *"Hey [Name], we had you down for [Time] today and didn't catch you — totally get it, life happens. Want me to put you on the calendar for later this week? Thursday or Friday?"*

**Soft lead:**
> *"Hey [Name], [You] from the other day. [Utility] just announced another rate hike — figured I'd loop you in. Worth a quick look now?"*

**Referral ask:**
> *"Hey [Name], hope the system's treating you right. Quick favor — anyone on your street you think we should talk to? We pay $[X] for every install referral."*

### The Door Re-Knock Rules

- Same door: max **3 times in 30 days**, different time of day each time.
- Always leave a card or door-hanger on the 1st and 2nd miss.
- 3rd visit: if no contact, archive for quarterly drip.

### The CRM Discipline

Every door, every conversation, logged within 1 hour. Notes must include:

- Name
- Best contact
- Utility bill amount
- Decision-maker status
- Specific objection (if any)
- Next action + date

If it's not in the CRM, it didn't happen.

## 15. Roleplay Library

Practice each scenario with a partner before going to the field. Switch roles after every rep.

### Scenario 1 – The Skeptical Boomer

**Setting:** 65-year-old man, arms crossed, "I've heard about solar — it's a scam."

**Your moves:**
- Don't argue. Validate.
- Drop a name of a neighbor on the street.
- Pivot to *"that's why we send out the consultant — you tell HIM no after 30 minutes if it doesn't make sense."*

**Sample dialogue:**

- *Homeowner:* "Solar's a scam. My buddy got ripped off."
- *Rep:* "Yeah, I hear that — and honestly, there ARE some shady companies. That's why I'm doing it door to door, not selling online. Did your buddy go with [bad company]?"
- *Homeowner:* "I dunno."
- *Rep:* "Yeah, most guys don't even know who installed theirs. We've put systems on three houses on this street — [#X] and the two at the corner. The consultant comes out, shows you the contract, you can shut it down in 5 minutes if it smells off. Worth half an hour Thursday or Friday?"

### Scenario 2 – The Busy Mom

**Setting:** Woman opens the door, baby on hip, looks exhausted.

**Your moves:**
- Be quick. Be respectful. Cut the pitch in half.
- Offer a phone callback at her convenience.

**Sample dialogue:**

- *Homeowner:* "I really can't talk right now."
- *Rep:* "100% — you've got your hands full. One yes/no question: are you guys paying over $150 a month to [Utility]?"
- *Homeowner:* "Yeah, way more."
- *Rep:* "Cool — what's the best number to text you? I'll text my name, and we'll set up a quick 5-minute call tomorrow with you AND your husband. No door visit unless you guys want one."

### Scenario 3 – The "Just Leave a Card" Guy

**Setting:** Cracks the door, doesn't open it all the way.

**Your moves:**
- Don't fight the door.
- Trade card for number.

### Scenario 4 – Both Spouses at the Door

**Setting:** Husband and wife both come to the door. **GREEN LIGHT — pitch hard.**

**Your moves:**
- Acknowledge BOTH by name and eye contact.
- Aim to set right now — they're both there. Could even pull the consultant in by phone.

### Scenario 5 – The Tire-Kicker Who Loves Talking

**Setting:** Will talk to you for an hour, won't commit.

**Your moves:**
- Be the one who closes the conversation.
- *"I love that you're into this — let's not waste your time at the door, let's get the consultant out so you can geek out with someone who actually engineers these systems. Thursday or Friday?"*

### Scenario 6 – The Hostile Door

**Setting:** "Get the f*** off my property."

**Your moves:**
- Smile. "Have a good night." Walk away. Do NOT respond.
- Do not record video. Do not argue. Do not slow down.
- Knock the next door within 60 seconds.

### Scenario 7 – Spanish-Speaking Household

**Setting:** Door opens, "No English."

**Your moves:**
- Smile. Try basic Spanish: *"Buenos días, ¿el dueño de la casa?"*
- If no fit, exit politely. Flag for bilingual rep on the team.

### Roleplay Drill: The Ladder

Daily morning drill with a partner. 15 minutes.

1. Round 1: Rep pitches perfectly. Partner gives no objections.
2. Round 2: Partner gives ONE objection from the list.
3. Round 3: Partner gives THREE objections in a row.
4. Round 4: Partner is hostile.
5. Round 5: Free-form, no script allowed.

Switch roles. Repeat.

## 16. The 30-Day Ramp Plan

### Week 1 – Absorb

- Shadow a top rep 3 days.
- Memorize the 5-Step Knock word-for-word.
- Knock 40 doors/day with a senior rep present.
- **Goal:** 1 appointment set.

### Week 2 – Reps

- Solo knocking.
- 60 doors/day minimum.
- Daily roleplay with manager.
- **Goal:** 4 appointments set, 2 sat.

### Week 3 – Volume

- 80 doors/day.
- Master the top 5 objections.
- Run your own confirmation texts.
- **Goal:** 8 appointments set, 5 sat, 1 close.

### Week 4 – Standard

- 80–100 doors/day.
- Hit weekly minimum (10 set, 7 sat, 2 close).
- Begin coaching newer reps.
- **Goal:** Hit "Setter" tier.

If you hit Week 4 targets, you graduate. If you don't, you re-run Weeks 2–3.

## 17. Quick Reference Cards

### Card A – The 5-Step Knock (Tape Inside Your iPad)

1. **Interrupt:** *"Sorry to bug you, I'm not selling anything door-to-door..."*
2. **Reason:** *"...new program with the utility..."*
3. **Micro-pitch:** *"...own your power, $0 down, neighbors got approved..."*
